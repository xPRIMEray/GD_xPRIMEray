# Overspace First Milestone — Approaching the Gallery Orb

**Hook:** Five frames of a camera moving through overspace toward a luminous orb — the first complete render of a space that doesn't follow ordinary geometry.

## Scientific Context

"Overspace" in xPRIMEray is a scene where the observer moves through a non-Euclidean environment: curved field paths, portal boundaries, and GRIN-mediated approach corridors. The milestone objective was to tune the auto-path camera motion toward a gallery orb and verify that curvature, overlay legibility, and framing all hold across the entire approach trajectory.

## Observation

Five evenly-spaced path-progress samples from 0% to 80%:

- **Start** — initial camera pose before auto-path begins. Baseline framing.
- **20%** — early approach. Field curvature is already deflecting peripheral rays. Overlay legibility is checked here.
- **40%** — mid-path. Orb alignment begins converging. The approach corridor's GRIN gradient is visible as frame compression.
- **60%** — late-mid approach. Motion should feel intentional. Transport structure begins composing around the orb.
- **80%** — near approach. The orb fills a significant portion of the frame; the surrounding field is strongly curved.

The contact sheet shows all five side-by-side, allowing camera speed, offset, and overlay density to be evaluated in one view.

## Why It Matters

This is xPRIMEray's first rendered milestone image in a real composite scene: not a test fixture or validation scene, but a *place* with visual intent. It proves the auto-path system, GRIN integration, and overlay pipeline can work together under a moving observer across a non-trivial trajectory.

## Next Step

Complete the 100% approach frame (orb entry). Extend the path to show the observer crossing the orb boundary — the first "entry into overspace" render. This sequence, completed, becomes the site's lead hero animation.
