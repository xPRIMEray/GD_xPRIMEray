# Wormhole Dual Reality — Six Steps from Perception to Interpretation

**Hook:** One wormhole, six frames — each adds one interpretive layer until the full transport picture is visible.

## Scientific Context

When a ray passes through a wormhole, it follows a curved trajectory that a straight-ray observer would not predict. The question this artifact addresses: how do you *show* that bending to someone who has only ever seen flat-ray renderers? The DualRealityTransport stack was built to answer this by composing multiple diagnostic overlays into a single coherent explanation ladder.

## Observation

The six-frame sequence reveals the wormhole's transport structure progressively:

1. **Clean curved** — the bare rendered image, no annotations. This is what an observer inside the wormhole would see.
2. **Reference Reality inset** — a frozen straight-ray render appears as an inset. The visual difference between the two exposes the distortion directly.
3. **Curvature heat map** — cumulative absolute ray-turn-angle mapped per pixel. The annular bright ring is where rays bend most — not at the throat, but at the portal boundary.
4. **Curvature + semantic glyphs** — BLV portal markers placed on the curvature map. The glyphs anchor the ring to physical geometry.
5. **Curvature + collision radar** — collision-aware labels added. High-curvature zones and collision-active zones do *not* fully overlap — this is a key transport insight.
6. **Full stack** — all layers simultaneously. The information-dense close for anyone who has followed the sequence.

## Why It Matters

This is xPRIMEray's clearest demonstration that transport interpretation is a layered process. A raw render hides the physics; each overlay makes one more layer of the transport structure legible. The progression from perception → comparison → measurement → geometry → collision → integration is the same logic used to design any transport diagnostic system.
